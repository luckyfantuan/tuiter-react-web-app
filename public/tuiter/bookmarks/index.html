There's no whiskey in mini bottles of Fireball, so customers are suing for fraud

Asia-Pacific stocks rise as Tokyo’s inflation nears 42-year high

Stocks in the Asia-Pacific traded higher on Friday as traders digested Tokyo's January core consumer prices that rose 4.3%, also faster than estimates — nearing the highest for Japan's capital since mid-1981. The Nikkei 225 was slightly above the flatline to end its session at 27,382.56 and the Topix gained 0.22% to close at 1,982.66. The yield on the 10-year Japanese government bond rose more than 3% to trade at 0.480%, inching close to the central bank's upper ceiling of its yield curve tolerance range.
